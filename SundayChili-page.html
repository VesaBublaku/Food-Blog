Sunday Chili

Servings: 7 servings
Prep Time : 15 minutes
Cook Time : 45-60 minutes
Total Time : 1 hour 15 minutes

Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat and add the ground beef, cooking and breaking it up with a spoon until browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes, then drain any excess fat and set the meat aside.

In the same pot, add the chopped onion, bell pepper, and minced garlic, sautéing for about 5 minutes until softened. Stir in the chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and ground cinnamon, and cook for 1 minute to toast the spices.

Add the diced tomatoes with their juices, tomato sauce, tomato paste, kidney beans, black beans, and pinto beans, and stir to combine. Pour in the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaf, and brown sugar (if using), then bring the chili to a simmer.

Reduce the heat to low and let the chili cook uncovered for 45 minutes to 1 hour, stirring occasionally, until the flavors have melded and the chili has thickened. Taste and adjust with salt and pepper as needed, adding extra broth or water if it’s too thick.

Once done, remove the bay leaf and serve the chili in bowls, topping with sour cream, shredded cheddar cheese, green onions, and cilantro, and enjoy with tortilla chips or cornbread.

Ingredients List

  • For the Chili
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 pound ground beef
    • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
    • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon chili powder/li>
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional, for added warmth)
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
    • 1 can (28 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
    • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
    • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 can (15 oz) pinto beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 cup beef broth (or vegetable broth)
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
    • Salt and black pepper to taste
    • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
    • 1 bay leaf
  • Optional Toppings
    • Sour cream
    • Shredded cheddar cheese
    • Sliced green onions
    • Chopped cilantro
    • Tortilla chips or cornbread for serving
