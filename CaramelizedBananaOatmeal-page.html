Caramelized Banana Oatmeal

Servings: 1-2
Prep Time : 5 minutes
Cook Time : 10 minutes
Total Time : 10-15 minutes

In a medium-sized saucepan, combine 1 cup of rolled oats, 2 cups of milk, maple syrup (if using), cinnamon, and a pinch of salt, and bring it to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.

Cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the oats are soft and have absorbed most of the liquid, then remove from heat and set aside. While the oats are cooking, heat 1 tablespoon of butter in a large skillet over medium heat, then add 2 sliced bananas to the skillet in a single layer.

Sprinkle with brown sugar and cinnamon, and cook for about 2-3 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the bananas are golden and caramelized. If desired, add vanilla extract for extra flavor.

Once the oatmeal and caramelized bananas are ready, spoon the oatmeal into bowls and top with the caramelized bananas.

Optionally, add toppings like chopped nuts, chia seeds, fresh fruit, yogurt, or peanut butter, and serve immediately while warm.

Ingredients List

  • For the Oatmeal
    • 1 cup rolled oats (or quick oats for a faster option)
    • 2 cups milk (any variety, such as cowâ€™s milk, almond milk)
    • 1 tbsp maple syrup (optional, for extra sweetness)
    • 1/2 tsp cinnamon (optional, for flavor)
    • Pinch of salt (to balance the sweetness)
  • For the Caramelized Bananas
    • 2 ripe bananas (sliced into rounds)
    • 1 tbsp butter (or coconut oil for a dairy-free option)
    • 1 tbsp brown sugar (or maple syrup)
    • 1/4 tsp cinnamon (optional, for extra flavor)
    • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (optional, for added depth)
  • Optional Toppings
    • Chopped nuts (such as walnuts, almonds, or pecans)
    • Chia seeds or flaxseeds
    • Sliced fresh fruit (such as strawberries, blueberries, or kiwi)
    • A dollop of yogurt (for added creaminess)
    • Drizzle of peanut butter or almond butter
