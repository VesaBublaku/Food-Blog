Maple-Mustard Tempeh Bowls

Servings: 4 servings
Prep Time : 10 minutes
Cook Time : 15-20
Total Time : 50-60 minutes

Begin by marinating the tempeh: whisk together 2 tablespoons of maple syrup, 1 1/2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon olive oil, 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar, 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder, 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional), and a pinch of salt and pepper in a bowl. Add the cubed tempeh, stirring to coat, and let it marinate for at least 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and spread 1 large cubed sweet potato and 1 cup halved Brussels sprouts on a baking sheet. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and toss to coat. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway, until vegetables are tender and browned. While the vegetables roast, rinse 1 cup quinoa, then add it to a saucepan with 2 cups water (or vegetable broth) and a pinch of salt.

Bring to a boil, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes until the quinoa is tender and liquid absorbed. In a large skillet over medium heat, add the marinated tempeh and any extra marinade, cooking for 7-10 minutes until caramelized and slightly crispy, adding a splash of water if needed to prevent sticking.

For the dressing, whisk together 2 tablespoons tahini, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, 1 tablespoon maple syrup, 1 tablespoon water (adjust to preferred consistency), 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder, and a pinch of salt and pepper until smooth.

Assemble the bowls by dividing the cooked quinoa among serving bowls and topping with the roasted sweet potatoes, Brussels sprouts, and maple-mustard tempeh. Drizzle the tahini dressing over the top and add any desired toppings, such as avocado slices, fresh parsley, or sesame seeds. Enjoy your flavorful and nutritious bowl!

Ingredients List

  • For the Maple-Mustard Tempeh
    • 8 ounces tempeh, cut into bite-sized cubes
    • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
    • 1 1/2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
    • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
    • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • For the Roasted Vegetables
    • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and cubed
    • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • For the Grain Base
    • 1 cup quinoa (or any preferred grain, such as brown rice or farro)
    • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
    • Pinch of salt
  • For the Creamy Tahini Dressing
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon water (adjust to desired consistency)
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Pinch of salt and pepper
  • Optional Toppings
    • Sliced avocado
    • Chopped fresh parsley or cilantro
    • Sesame seeds or hemp seeds
