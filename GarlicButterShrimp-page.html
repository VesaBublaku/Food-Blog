Garlic Butter Shrimp

Servings: 3 servings
Prep Time : 10-15 minutes
Cook Time : 10-15 minutes
Total Time : 20-30 minutes

Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels and season with salt, pepper, and paprika. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat, then add the shrimp in a single layer and cook for 1–2 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and opaque.

Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and add 1 tablespoon of olive oil and the butter. Once the butter melts, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, until fragrant.

Add the red pepper flakes (if using) and cook for another 30 seconds, then pour in the chicken broth or white wine, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom of the skillet. Let the sauce simmer for 2–3 minutes until slightly reduced.

Return the shrimp to the skillet, tossing to coat them in the garlic butter sauce, and heat for another 1–2 minutes.

Squeeze in the lemon juice, stir in the chopped parsley, and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Serve the shrimp with extra lemon wedges and optionally sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients List

  • For the Shrimp
    • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    • 1 tablespoon paprika (optional, for a little color and flavor)
  • For the Garlic Butter Sauce
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 6 cloves garlic, minced (or more, depending on preference)
    • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)
    • 1/4 cup chicken broth or dry white wine (optional)
    • Juice of 1 lemon (plus lemon wedges for serving)
    • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
    • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for topping)
