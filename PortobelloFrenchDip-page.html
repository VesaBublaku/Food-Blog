Portobello French Dip

Servings: 4 servings
Prep Time : 15 minutes
Cook Time : 20 minutes
Total Time : 35 minutes

To make the Portobello French Dip, start by heating 2 tbsp olive oil and 1 tbsp butter in a large skillet over medium heat.

Add the sliced onions and cook for 5-7 minutes until softened and caramelized. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Next, add the sliced portobello mushrooms, 1 tbsp soy sauce, salt, pepper, 1/2 tsp thyme, and 1/2 tsp rosemary, stirring well to coat.

Cook for 7-10 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and have released their juices, then remove from heat. To prepare the au jus, combine 2 cups vegetable broth, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce, 1/2 tsp thyme, 1/2 tsp rosemary, and salt and pepper in a saucepan, bringing it to a simmer over medium heat for 5-7 minutes.

For assembly, split the hoagie rolls and butter each side, toasting them in a skillet or under the broiler until golden. Place a slice of provolone cheese on each roll (if using), then top with the mushroom and onion mixture.

Serve each sandwich with a small bowl of the au jus for dipping, and enjoy this flavorful, hearty vegetarian French dip.

Ingredients List

  • For the Portobello Filling
    • 4 large portobello mushrooms, stems removed and sliced
    • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 1 tbsp butter
    • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tbsp soy sauce
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1/2 tsp thyme
    • 1/2 tsp rosemary
  • For the Au Jus
    • 2 cups vegetable broth
    • 1 tbsp soy sauce
    • 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
    • 11/2 tsp thyme
    • 1/2 tsp rosemary
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For Assembly
    • 4 hoagie rolls or French rolls
    • 4 slices provolone cheese
    • Butter for toasting rolls
