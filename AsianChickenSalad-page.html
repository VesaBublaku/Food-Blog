Asian Chicken Salad

Servings: 7 servings
Prep Time : 15-20 minutes
Cook Time : 15-20 minutes
Total Time : 45-60 minutes

To make the Asian Chicken Salad, start by marinating the chicken breasts in a mixture of soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and black pepper for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours).

After marinating, grill or pan-cook the chicken for 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked through, then let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing thinly against the grain. While the chicken cooks, prepare the vegetables by shredding the cabbage, slicing the cucumber and bell pepper, and chopping the cilantro and green onions, then place them in a large bowl with the mixed greens.

Toast the cashews or almonds and sesame seeds in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes if using. For the dressing, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, lime juice, peanut butter (optional), and chili paste or sriracha (optional) in a bowl or jar, adjusting with water if necessary for the right consistency.

Once the chicken is ready and sliced, add it to the vegetables and toss everything with the dressing.

Garnish with toasted nuts, sesame seeds, cilantro, and crispy chow mein noodles if desired, then serve immediately or refrigerate for later.

Ingredients List

  • For the Salad
    • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
    • 6 cups mixed greens (such as romaine, arugula, or spinach)
    • 1 cup shredded cabbage (purple or napa cabbage, or a mix of both)
    • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
    • 1/2 cup red bell pepper, julienned
    • 1/2 cup cucumber, thinly sliced
    • 1/4 cup sliced green onions (scallions)
    • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
    • 1/4 cup roasted or salted cashews (or almonds for variation)
    • 1/4 cup sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)
    • 1/4 cup crispy chow mein noodles (optional, for crunch)
  • For the Chicken Marinade
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
    • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • For the Dressing
    • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
    • 1 teaspoon fresh grated ginger
    • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon lime juice
    • 1 tablespoon creamy peanut butter (optional)
    • 1-2 teaspoons chili paste or sriracha
    • Salt and pepper to taste
