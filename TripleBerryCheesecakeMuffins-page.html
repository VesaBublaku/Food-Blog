Triple Berry Cheesecake Muffins

Servings: 3-5
Prep Time : 15 minutes
Cook Time : 18-22 minutes
Total Time : 45-50 minutes

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon (if using).

In a separate bowl, whisk the egg, milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined, then pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Gently fold in the mixed berries and white chocolate chips (if using).

In another bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth, then add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and egg, and continue beating until fully combined. Spoon about 2 tablespoons of muffin batter into each muffin cup, then add a heaping tablespoon of the cheesecake filling on top of the batter, followed by another tablespoon of muffin batter, covering the cheesecake layer.

Bake for 18-22 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the muffin comes out clean (except for cheesecake filling), and the tops are golden brown.

Let the muffins cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar and garnish with fresh berries before serving.

Ingredients List

  • For the Muffins
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
    • 1/4 tsp baking soda
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (optional, for flavor)
    • 1 large egg
    • 1/2 cup whole milk (or any milk of your choice)
    • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1/2 cup mixed fresh berries
    • 1/4 cup white chocolate chips (optional, for extra sweetness)
  • For the Cheesecake Filling
    • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
    • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1 large egg
  • Optional Toppings
    • Fresh berries (for garnish)
    • Powdered sugar, for dusting
