Burrata Bruschetta

Servings: 2-5
Prep Time : 5 minutes
Cook Time : 8-10 minutes
Total Time : 15 minutes

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and arrange the bread slices on a baking sheet, brushing each slice lightly with olive oil. Toast the bread for about 8-10 minutes until golden brown and crispy; if desired, rub each slice with a garlic clove after toasting for extra flavor.

Meanwhile, slice or gently tear the burrata and drain any excess liquid. In a small bowl, season the halved or quartered cherry tomatoes with a bit of salt and pepper.

Once the bread is toasted, place a portion of burrata on each slice, top with cherry tomatoes, and add a few basil leaves.

Drizzle with balsamic glaze, sprinkle with additional salt and pepper if desired, and garnish with microgreens, arugula, or a touch of lemon zest.

Serve immediately while the bread is still warm to enjoy the full contrast of textures and flavors.

Ingredients List

  • Bread Base
    • Sourdough or baguette slices – 1 loaf (cut into 1/2-inch thick slices)
    • Olive oil – 2-3 tablespoons (for brushing)
  • Toppings
    • Fresh burrata cheese – 8 oz (about 2 large burrata balls)
    • Cherry tomatoes – 1 cup (halved or quartered)
    • Fresh basil leaves – 1/4 cup (torn or thinly sliced)
    • Balsamic glaze – 2 tablespoons (for drizzling)
    • Sea salt – to taste
    • Freshly ground black pepper – to taste
  • Optional Garnishes
    • Microgreens or arugula – small handful (for a peppery touch)
    • Garlic clove – 1 large (for rubbing on bread slices after toasting)
    • Lemon zest – 1 teaspoon (for a hint of citrus)
