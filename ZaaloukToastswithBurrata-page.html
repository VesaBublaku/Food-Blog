Zaalouk Toasts with Burrata

Servings: 6 - 8 toasts
Prep Time : 15 minutes
Cook Time : 40 minutes
Total Time : 55 minutes

To make Zaalouk toasts with burrata, start by making the zaalouk, a Moroccan eggplant and tomato salad.

Begin by roasting 2 medium eggplants in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for 30-40 minutes until soft and the skin is charred. Once roasted, peel the eggplants, discard the skin, and chop the flesh. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 onion (chopped) and sauté for 5 minutes until softened.

Add 3 cloves of minced garlic, 2 medium tomatoes (chopped), 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon paprika, 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander, and a pinch of cayenne pepper for heat. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and the mixture becomes saucy.

Stir in the roasted eggplant and cook for another 5 minutes, mashing it slightly with the back of a spoon to incorporate. Season with salt, pepper, and a handful of chopped fresh cilantro.

For the toasts, slice a baguette or your choice of rustic bread into 1-inch slices. Brush each slice lightly with olive oil and toast them in a pan or under the broiler until golden brown.

To assemble, spread a generous amount of the zaalouk on each toast, top with a ball of fresh burrata cheese, and drizzle with olive oil. Garnish with extra fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro, and a sprinkle of salt and pepper. Serve immediately as an appetizer or a light meal.

Ingredients List

  • For the Zaalouk
    • 2 medium eggplants
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon cumin
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh cilantro
  • For the Toasts
    • 1 baguette or rustic bread
    • Olive oil
    • 1 ball burrata cheese
    • Fresh parsley or cilantro
