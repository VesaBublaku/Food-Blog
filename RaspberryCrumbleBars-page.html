Raspberry Crumble Bars

Servings: 1-3
Prep Time : 15-20 minutes
Cook Time : 35-40 minutes
Total Time : 1 hour 15 minutes

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper or grease it. For the raspberry filling, combine the raspberries, sugar, lemon juice, and cornstarch in a medium saucepan.

Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the mixture simmers and thickens, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract, then set aside to cool.

For the crust and topping, mix the flour, oats, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon in a large bowl. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to cut it into the dry ingredients until it forms a crumbly mixture. Beat the egg and add it to the mixture, stirring until the dough comes together but remains crumbly.

Press about 2/3 of the dough into the bottom of the prepared pan, forming the crust. Pour the cooled raspberry filling over the crust and spread it evenly. Crumble the remaining dough mixture over the top of the filling.

Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Let the bars cool completely in the pan, then lift them out using the parchment paper and cut them into squares. Enjoy!

Ingredients List

  • For the Crust and Crumble Topping
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
    • 1 large egg (for the dough)
  • For the Raspberry Filling
    • 2 1/2 cups fresh raspberries (or frozen, thawed)
    • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional, for extra flavor)
