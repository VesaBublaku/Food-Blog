Creamy Red Pepper Pasta

Servings: 4
Prep Time : 15 minutes
Cook Time : 25 - 30 minutes
Total Time : 40 - 45 minutes

To make Creamy Red Pepper Pasta, begin by roasting the red bell peppers at 450°F (230°C) for about 20-25 minutes, or until the skin is blackened and blistered.

You can also roast them on the stovetop or grill for a smoky flavor. Once roasted, place the peppers in a bowl and cover with plastic wrap for 10 minutes to allow them to steam, which makes the skins easier to peel off. After peeling away the skin and removing the seeds, set the peppers aside.

Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Be sure to reserve about ½ cup of the pasta water before draining the pasta. For the sauce, place the roasted red peppers, minced garlic, heavy cream, vegetable broth, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper into a blender or food processor

Blend until the mixture is smooth and creamy. If the sauce seems too thick, you can add a bit of the reserved pasta water to achieve your desired consistency. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat and pour in the creamy red pepper sauce, simmering for a few minutes to heat it through.

Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, tossing it gently to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce. If the sauce needs thinning, stir in more reserved pasta water. Once everything is well-combined and heated, serve the pasta in bowls, garnishing with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped basil for a burst of freshness.

This creamy, flavorful pasta makes for a satisfying and indulgent meal that’s perfect for a cozy dinner.

Ingredients List

  • For the Sauce
    • 2 large red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • ½ cup vegetable or chicken broth
    • ½ tsp smoked paprika
    • Salt and pepper
    • 1 tbsp fresh basil, chopped
  • For the Pasta
    • 12 oz pasta
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • Fresh Parmesan cheese (optional, for serving)
