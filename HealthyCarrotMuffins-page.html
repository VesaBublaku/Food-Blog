Healthy Carrot Muffins

Servings: 4-7
Prep Time : 10-15 minutes
Cook Time : 35-40 minutes
Total Time : 1-1.5 hours

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly. In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and oats (if using).

In another bowl, whisk together the egg, applesauce, maple syrup, vanilla extract, milk, and olive or coconut oil until smooth. Stir the grated carrots into the wet ingredients and add any optional add-ins like chopped walnuts, raisins, or chia seeds.

Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and gently fold until just combined, being careful not to overmix.

Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full, and bake for 18-22 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Let the muffins cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy warm or at room temperature as a delicious, healthy snack or breakfast!

Ingredients List

  • Dry Ingredients
    • Whole wheat flour – 1 1/2 cups
    • Baking powder – 1 teaspoon
    • Baking soda – 1/2 teaspoon
    • Ground cinnamon – 1 teaspoon
    • Ground nutmeg – 1/4 teaspoon (optional, for extra warmth)
    • Salt – 1/4 teaspoon
    • Oats – 1/2 cup (rolled oats, optional for added texture)
  • Wet Ingredients
    • Egg – 1 large
    • Unsweetened applesauce – 1/2 cup
    • Maple syrup – 1/4 cup (or honey)
    • Vanilla extract – 1 teaspoon
    • Milk – 1/4 cup (any milk of choice, almond or oat milk)
    • Olive oil or coconut oil – 2 tablespoons (for healthy fats)
  • For the Quiche Custard
    • Eggs – 4 large
    • Heavy cream – 1 cup (or use half-and-half for a lighter version)
    • Milk – 1/2 cup
    • Ground nutmeg – a pinch (optional, for extra flavor)
    • Cheese – 1 1/2 cups (grated; such as cheddar, Gruyère, or a mix of both)
  • Add-ins
    • Carrots – 2 medium (finely grated)
    • Chopped walnuts or almonds – 1/3 cup (optional, for added crunch)
    • Raisins or currants – 1/4 cup (optional, for sweetness)
    • Chia seeds or flaxseeds – 1 tablespoon (optional, for extra nutrition)
