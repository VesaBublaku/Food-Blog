Instant Pot Wild Rice Soup

Servings: 6 - 8 servings
Prep Time : 10 minutes
Cook Time : 35 minutes
Total Time : 45 minutes

For a hearty and comforting Instant Pot wild rice soup, start by setting your Instant Pot to the sauté function.

Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil, then sauté 1 chopped onion, 2 carrots (diced), and 2 celery stalks (diced) for 5-6 minutes, until softened. Add 3 cloves of minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds. Stir in 1 1/2 cups of wild rice, 1 teaspoon dried thyme, 1 teaspoon dried sage, and 1/2 teaspoon salt.

Pour in 6 cups of vegetable broth (or chicken broth if preferred), and add 1 bay leaf. Secure the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 35 minutes.

After the cooking time is up, allow for a natural release for about 10 minutes, then do a quick release for any remaining pressure.

Once the pressure is released, open the lid and stir in 1 cup of heavy cream or half-and-half, and 1/2 cup of frozen peas (optional for color and sweetness). Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

For a thicker soup, you can mash a portion of the rice with a potato masher or immersion blender. Serve warm, garnished with fresh parsley if desired.

Ingredients List

  • For the Soup
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 2 carrots, diced
    • 2 carrots, diced
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 1/2 cups wild rice
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • 1 teaspoon dried sage
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half
    • 1/2 cup frozen peas
    • Salt and pepper
    • Fresh parsley
