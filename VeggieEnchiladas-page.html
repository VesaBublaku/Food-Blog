Veggie Enchiladas

Servings: 3-6 servings
Prep Time : 20 minutes
Cook Time : 40 minutes
Total Time : 1 hour

To make the Veggie Enchiladas, start by preparing the enchilada sauce: heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a medium saucepan, add the chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, and cayenne (if using), and cook for 1 minute, stirring to toast the spices.

Add the tomato sauce and vegetable broth, then simmer for 5-10 minutes, seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat and sauté the onion and bell pepper for 3-4 minutes.

Add the zucchini and cook for another 2-3 minutes, then stir in the corn, black beans, spinach, green chilies, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper, cooking for 3-4 more minutes until the spinach wilts. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

To assemble, spread 1/2 cup of the enchilada sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish, warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave, and spoon about 1/4 cup of the veggie mixture and some shredded cheese onto each tortilla.

Roll them up tightly and place them seam-side down in the baking dish. Pour the remaining sauce over the tortillas and top with the remaining cheese. Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden. Let the enchiladas sit for 5 minutes before serving, garnishing with cilantro and lime wedges.

Ingredients List

  • For the Enchiladas
    • 12 small corn tortillas
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 1 red bell pepper, diced
    • 1 zucchini, diced
    • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
    • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
    • 1 can (4 oz) diced green chilies (optional, for added heat)
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • 2 cups shredded cheese
    • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
    • 1 lime, cut into wedges (for serving)
  • For the Homemade Enchilada Sauce
    • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
    • 1/2 cup vegetable broth (or water)
    • 1 tablespoon chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
