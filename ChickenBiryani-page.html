Chicken Biryani

Servings: 4-6
Prep Time : 30 minutes
Cook Time : 60 minutes
Total Time : 1.5 - 2 hours

To make this chicken biryani, start by marinating the chicken. In a large mixing bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, ground cumin, ground coriander, salt, and lemon juice.

Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade, covering and refrigerating for at least an hour, or ideally overnight, to infuse the flavors deeply.

While the chicken marinates, prepare the rice. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, adding the soaked, drained basmati rice along with the bay leaf, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, cloves, and salt. Cook the rice for about 5-6 minutes until it’s 70-80% done, meaning the grains are firm but not fully cooked. Drain the rice and set it aside.

Next, in a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they turn golden brown and caramelized, which takes about 10-15 minutes.

Be careful not to let them burn, lowering the heat if necessary. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnishing.

In the same skillet with the remaining onions, add the marinated chicken along with any remaining marinade. Sauté the chicken on medium heat for about 5-7 minutes until it starts to cook through and the spices release their aroma

Then, reduce the heat, cover the skillet, and let the chicken cook for an additional 10-12 minutes until tender. Once cooked, remove it from the heat.

To assemble the biryani layers, preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) or prepare for stovetop dum cooking. In the same pot with the cooked chicken, add half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken as the first rice layer. Sprinkle half of the fresh mint, cilantro, fried onions, and green chilies over this layer. Add the remaining rice on top, then add the rest of the mint, cilantro, fried onions, and saffron milk if using, for extra flavor and color

For dum cooking, cover the pot tightly with aluminum foil or a lid to seal in the steam. If you’re using an oven, place the pot in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes. If cooking on the stovetop, place the pot over very low heat and steam it for about 20-25 minutes, using a heat diffuser if needed

Once done, remove the lid and gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice grains. Serve the biryani hot, garnished with extra cilantro or mint if desired, along with a side of raita for a refreshing contrast. Enjoy this rich, aromatic chicken biryani!

Ingredients List

  • For the Marinade
    • 1 lb (500g) chicken, bone-in pieces
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • Salt to taste
    • Juice of 1 lemon
  • For the Rice
    • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked and rinsed
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 3-4 green cardamom pods
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 4-5 whole cloves
  • For the Biryani Layers
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
    • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
    • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
    • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
    • 2-3 green chilies, sliced lengthwise (optional)
