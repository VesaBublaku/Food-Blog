Swedish Meatballs

Servings: 4 servings
Prep Time : 15 minutes
Cook Time : 3 hours
Total Time : 3 hours 15 minutes

To make the Swedish Meatballs, start by combining 1 lb ground beef, 1/2 lb ground pork, 1/2 cup breadcrumbs, 1/4 cup milk, 1 finely chopped onion, 1 egg,

1/2 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp pepper, 1/4 tsp allspice, and 1/4 tsp nutmeg in a large bowl, mixing until just combined. Roll the mixture into 1-inch meatballs. In a large skillet, melt 1 tbsp butter over medium heat and cook the meatballs in batches until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes, then transfer to a plate.

For the sauce, melt 2 tbsp butter in the skillet, then whisk in 2 tbsp flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until lightly golden. Gradually whisk in 2 cups beef broth, stirring to smooth out any lumps, and bring to a simmer.

Add 1 cup heavy cream and 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce, seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer the sauce for 5-7 minutes until thickened. Add the meatballs back into the sauce and cook for another 5 minutes until heated through.

Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm over mashed potatoes, noodles, or rice.

Ingredients List

  • For the Meatballs
    • 1 lb ground beef
    • 1/2 lb ground pork
    • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
    • 1/4 cup milk
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 1 egg
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 21/4 tsp black pepper
    • 1/4 tsp ground allspice
    • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
    • 1 tbsp butter
  • For the Sauce
    • 2 tbsp butter
    • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
    • 2 cups beef broth
    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 21 tsp Worcestershire sauce
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh parsley, chopped
