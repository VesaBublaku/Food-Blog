Double Chocolate Cookies

Servings: 3-6
Prep Time : 15-20 minutes
Cook Time : 9-11 minutes
Total Time : 1 hour

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two baking sheets with parchment paper. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt, then set aside.

In a large bowl, cream the softened butter with the granulated sugar and brown sugar using an electric mixer on medium speed until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition, and stir in the vanilla extract.

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips and chopped chocolate.

Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough and roll them into balls, then optionally roll them in granulated sugar before placing them about 2 inches apart on the prepared baking sheets.

Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still soft. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy!

Ingredients List

  • For the Cookie Dough
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
    • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
    • 1 cup pistachios, shelled and chopped finely
    • 1 1/2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
    • 1/2 cup chopped chocolate (optional, for extra richness)
  • Optional for Rolling
    • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for rolling the dough balls)
