Smash Burger

Servings: 4 - 6 servings
Prep Time : 10 minutes
Cook Time : 25 - 30 minutes
Total Time : 35 - 40 minutes

To make Smash Burgers, start by dividing 1 lb of ground beef into 4 equal portions (about 4 oz each) and gently rolling each portion into a loose ball without overworking the meat.

Heat a large cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat for about 3-4 minutes, then add 1 tbsp of vegetable oil, swirling it to coat the pan evenly. Once the skillet is hot, place the beef balls onto the skillet and, using a heavy spatula or a burger press, smash each ball firmly into a thin patty, making sure to press down hard enough to achieve a good sear and crispy edges but not so much that the patty breaks apart.

Cook the patties for about 2-3 minutes until the edges start to crisp up and a nice golden-brown crust forms. Flip the patties and immediately place a slice of cheese on top, allowing it to melt as the patty cooks for another 1-2 minutes, or until the burger reaches an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C) for well-done.

While the burgers are cooking, toast the buns in the same skillet or on a separate pan for 1-2 minutes until golden and slightly crispy.

To assemble the burgers, place the cooked patties on the bottom halves of the toasted buns, then top with your favorite toppings such as lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, red onions, ketchup, mustard, or any other condiments you prefer. Place the top bun on the burgers, serve immediately, and enjoy your juicy, crispy Smash Burgers!

Ingredients List

  • For the Burgers
    • 1 lb ground beef
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 4 burger buns
    • 4 slices American cheese
    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Toppings
    • Lettuce
    • Tomato slices
    • Pickles
    • Red onion slices
    • Ketchup and mustard
