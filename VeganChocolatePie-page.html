Vegan Chocolate Pie

Servings: 5-9
Prep Time : 20-25 minutes
Cook Time : 8-10 minutes
Total Time : 3-4 hours

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a medium bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, melted coconut oil, and maple syrup until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand, then press this mixture firmly into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie dish to form the crust.

Bake the crust for 8-10 minutes until lightly golden and firm, then let it cool completely. In a small saucepan, heat the coconut milk over medium heat until it starts to simmer, but don’t let it boil; for a thicker filling, whisk in cornstarch or arrowroot powder and cook, stirring, for 1-2 minutes until slightly thickened.

Add the maple syrup and salt, then remove the saucepan from heat. Add the chopped dark chocolate to the warm coconut milk, let it sit for a minute, then whisk until the chocolate is fully melted and the mixture is smooth and glossy, and stir in the vanilla extract.

Pour the chocolate filling into the cooled crust, spreading it evenly with a spatula and tapping the dish gently on the counter to release any air bubbles.

Place the pie in the refrigerator and chill for at least 3-4 hours, or overnight, until the filling is set, then serve with your choice of toppings, such as vegan whipped cream, fresh berries, shaved chocolate, or chopped nuts.

Ingredients List

  • For the Crust
    • 1 1/2 cups vegan graham cracker crumbs (or gluten-free if desired)
    • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted (or vegan butter, melted)
    • 2 tablespoons maple syrup or agave syrup
  • For the Chocolate Filling
    • 1 1/4 cups full-fat coconut milk (from a can, for creaminess)
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch or arrowroot powder (optional)
    • 1/4 cup maple syrup or agave syrup (adjust for sweetness)
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 10 ounces dairy-free dark chocolate, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional Toppings
    • Vegan whipped cream
    • Fresh berries (like raspberries or strawberries)
    • Shaved dark chocolate or cocoa powder
    • Chopped nuts (e.g., almonds or hazelnuts)
