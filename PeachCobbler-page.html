Peach Cobbler

Servings: 1-2 servings
Prep Time : 20-25 minutes
Cook Time : 40-45 minutes
Total Time : 1-1 hour 10 minutes

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish. In a large bowl, toss the peaches with lemon juice, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to form a slurry and stir it into the peach mixture.

Pour the peach mixture into the prepared baking dish. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon. Add the cold, cubed butter and work it into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs.

Stir in the buttermilk until the dough just comes together. Drop spoonfuls of the biscuit dough over the peaches, leaving some gaps between the spoonfuls.

Optionally, sprinkle a little sugar over the topping. Bake for 40–45 minutes, or until the peach filling is bubbling and the biscuit topping is golden brown.

Allow the cobbler to cool for 10–15 minutes before serving, and optionally top with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.

Ingredients List

  • For the Peach Filling
    • 6 cups fresh peaches, peeled, pitted, and sliced
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1/4 cup brown sugar, packed
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for thickening)
    • Pinch of salt
  • For the Biscuit Topping
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
    • 2/3 cup buttermilk
  • For Serving (optional)
    • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream
